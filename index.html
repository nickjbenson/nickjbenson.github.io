Kami is a musical stand-in-one-spot-and-wave-your-hands-around simulator for Oculus VR and Leap Motion.

Sadly, you cannot play Kami as a VR experience, because browser VR doesn't really exist yet, and ain't nobody got VR equipment lying around. Instead, you can play a WebGL port using the mouse to look, the left button to pull on critters, and the right button to push critters away.

Final project for 21M.359 Interactive Music Systems, Fall 2016. (Originally titled "Musicritters.")

The Little Things was a parody fighting game designed to bring attention to "micro-aggressions," or, those moments when one subjects another to the judgements of social, systemic bias, often without even realizing it, and usually pertaining to gender, race, or another social construction of identity.

Girl gamers get a lot of flak for their gender, but guy gamers don't realize when they're being digglyboops. So the Little Team (Ilana Habib, Royal Morris, Liz Rita, Nick Benson) aimed to make a fighting game that could have fun and subvert the usual gender bullywhoozit at the same time.

In CMS.610, game projects were developed into brief demos and supplementary material to pitch to a panel of industry veterans.

Reverb was a multiplayer co-op platform puzzle game about navigating a dark, unfamiliar world illuminated by sound rather than light. I worked on the project as Level and Gameplay Designer.

Work progressed smoothly until the second round of playtesting. Set in the same network environment as that of the final, live demo, we observed buggy, lag-induced behavior that broke the play experience, which was intended for two cooperating players who could communicate with one another.

We were unable to solve our client synchronization issues in the final weeks, leaving us with an unreliable play experience too close to our final deadline. In the end, we converted the game into a single-player museum, taking advantage of the scenery we did have to discuss the various challenges of the project and where it went wrong.
